# Risk And QA

Non-negotiable rules:

- Do not invent product facts.
- Do not treat competitor images as user-owned source product images.
- Do not directly copy competitor visual composition, layout, claims, or brand styling.
- Preserve source product identity: silhouette, proportions, color family, material appearance, texture, hardware, closure, straps/handles, accessories, logo/markings, and distinctive details.
- Require evidence for certification, safety, medical, waterproof, fireproof, child-safe, pet-safe, FDA, clinically proven, or similar claims.
- Do not auto-publish or auto-upload to marketplaces.
- Do not promise CTR, CVR, ROAS, ACOS, ranking, or sales lift.
- Run QA before final export.
- Treat canvas annotations or user revision notes as structured revision input.

Human confirmation is required for:

- Certification claims.
- Safety, medical, child, pet, electrical, or efficacy claims.
- Direct comparison claims against competitors.
- Brand logos and trademarks.
- High-regulation categories.

QA checklist:

- Product identity remains consistent.
- Product Identity Lock exists for final product-bearing generated images.
- Generated images are reviewed side-by-side against source/enhanced source image.
- Detail closeups can be traced to visible source evidence.
- No invented function, material, accessory, specification, certification, or package content appears.
- Copy stays inside the Product Fact Sheet evidence boundary.
- High-risk terms are removed or flagged.
- Image roles match the target platform.
- Localized copy is natural for the target market.
- Every image in the set has a clear role.
- Competitor references are used only for analysis and differentiation.
- User confirmation needs are clearly listed.
